Making an Appointment

Appointments can be scheduled up to two weeks in advance. We ask that all adopters be fully prepared to take a dog home immediately after the appointment. Please note that we do not hold dogs unless they require further veterinary care.

If you have upcoming travel and are concerned about disrupting your new pet's adjustment period, please schedule your appointment for after your return.

If you don't find a match on your first visit, you are welcome to schedule follow-up appointments until you do.

Adopting a dog is a significant decision. To ensure the best possible outcome for both you and the dog, we require that adopters personally attend the appointment and make the selection themselves, rather than allowing friends or neighbors to make such a decision. We also do not allow pre-paying for a dog you have not yet met in-person.

To make your appointment, visit our volunteer-managed scheduling portal at www.savinggracencscheduler.com. If clicking on this link doesn't work, try typing this website into the browser searchbar. Keep your email open! You'll need to check it again for a one-time passcode. If you don't see it come within 2 minutes, check your junk and spam folders and see if your passcode was sent there.

If you experience any issues with the scheduling portal, please don't hesitate to reach out to us at adoptions@savinggracenc.org, but please provide specific details about what went wrong. Simply saying "it's not working" really doesn't give us enough information to start looking into what might be going wrong. (If possible, including a screenshot would be especially helpful!)

We schedule appointments up to two weeks in advance. If you're planning ahead, your application will remain on file for up to one year after approval. The only follow-up action required is to schedule your appointment through our portal as instructed above.

Availability of Dogs

Appointments typically last about an hour, during which you will meet several dogs available at that time. Any dog listed on our website is currently available, and if a dog is no longer listed, they have already found their new home.

Our dogs are available on a first-come, first-served basis. We do not hold dogs for specific appointments. Having an appointment does not guarantee that any specific dog will be available for you to meet. Unfortunately, due to volunteer capacity, we are unable to track requests or wishlists by email.

The more flexible you are during your visit, the more opportunities you'll have to find the right match. You're welcome to monitor our website before your visit, as we try to update it daily. Keep in mind that we often have more dogs available than are listed online, so a visit is always a good idea.

While email is our preferred method for communicating policies, the best way to inquire about specific dogs is to speak directly with the counselor you meet during your appointment. We generally do not recommend dogs ahead of your visit.

If you're interested in PUPPIES (up to around 5 months old), they typically arrive on Wednesdays. If a puppy's bio lists a specific date, that's the day they become available. They must be adopted the same day they are made available and cannot be picked up at a later date. Please note, we cannot guarantee the size of puppies as we do not know their father's identity.

If you're looking for SMALL ADULTS (under 20 lbs), please plan to schedule your visit on a Friday or Saturday, as these dogs are cared for in foster homes and only available for appointments on these days.

We do not offer trial adoptions. You may have heard of our Host Weekend program, which allows some of our long-term residents to enjoy a break from the shelter. While it's not a trial adoption, Host Weekend is a great way to see if dog ownership is the right fit for you. Please note, requests for specific dogs cannot be honored for Host Weekend.

Our Location

Saving Grace is located at 13400 Old Creedmoor Rd, Wake Forest, NC. All adoption appointments are held at this location. Please note that we **do not** offer transportation or shipping of dogs.

We are happy to accommodate guests with accessibility needs. Please inform us of any special requirements when scheduling your appointment, so we can ensure the best experience for you and your future pet.

Contacting Us

Our Adoptions Team is busy interacting with dogs and adopters during operating hours. Email is the best way to stay in touch with us, as our phone line is not actively monitored. Several volunteers assist in managing email communication, which helps ensure that we are all on the same page and can provide timely responses.

Getting Ready for Your Appointment

We encourage you to bring all members of your household who will be involved in the dog's care, including children, roommates, or spouses. It's particularly important that children attend, as they can be unpredictable, and the dog should feel comfortable with everyone in the household. Some dogs may not be compatible with young children, so it's best to make this a family decision.

You're welcome to bring your current dog, but please note that we do not have off-leash areas for meet-and-greets. All introductions will take place on leash in the parking lot. During warmer weather, your dog will need to wait in the parking lot with a family member. If it's cool enough, your dog may wait in your car while you meet potential new pets. Please let us know in advance if you plan to bring your dog so we can make preparations.

Adoption Outcomes and Fees

The adoption fee is $395, and we accept cash or credit/debit cards (we do not accept checks or split payments). Please bring a valid ID. We recommend bringing exact cash unless you wish to make a donation, as we do not keep change on-site.

You are under no obligation to adopt during your visit, but if you decide to proceed, here are the three possible outcomes:

  1. ADOPTABLE: If the dog is ready for adoption, you may take them home the same day.
  2. FOSTER TO ADOPT (FTA Program): If the dog is heartworm-positive, they will go home with you as a foster until treatment is completed. Final adoption will take place after the third treatment, usually within 8-10 weeks.
  3. RESERVED/CHOSEN: Some dogs may require additional veterinary care before they are ready for adoption. These dogs are "chosen" and will not be available for other adopters until they are cleared.

Partnerships

We are proud to partner with Boehringer-Ingelheim (BI), who offers a free preventative care program for adopted dogs. BI will contact you after adoption with more information about their services, and you will have the option to opt out of future communications.

Additionally, we use a private scheduling program owned and created by one of our volunteers. Your application data will be stored securely in the database and used solely for scheduling purposes.
